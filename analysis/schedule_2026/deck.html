Methodology

One lens, applied sixteen times.

How the SoS metric — and everything downstream — is computed.

Strength of Schedule (SoS) is derived from Vegas season win totals: each team's win total is their implied wins. A team's SoS = the sum (or average) of their 17 opponents' implied wins. This is the canonical lens used across every SoS-flavored analysis in this deck.

Win totals source: Rotowire / DraftKings as of 2026-05-15 (one day after schedule release — lines will move).

Schedule source: nflverse games.csv — full 272-game 2026 slate.

Other inputs: nflverse play-by-play 2020-2025 (pace, return-week retrospective), nflverse rosters (player movement), meteostat (stadium climate), Ourlads (depth charts).

IStrength of Schedule

How hard is hard,
and how much of it is real?

Vegas win totals give every opponent a single number. Sum them across seventeen games and you have a schedule's weight — but the same total can hide a back-loaded slope, a four-week gauntlet, or pure scheduling noise. Four angles.

Implied Wins SoS.

  • Hardest schedule: ARI (156.5 opp wins, +0.64 vs avg)
  • Top 5: ARI · MIA (152.5) · DAL · ATL · LA · CAR (150.5)
  • Easiest: DET (135.5, -0.59 vs avg)
  • Bottom 5: DET · NO (137.5) · CIN · KC (138.5) · CLE · NYJ · SF (140.5)

How to read Each team's SoS is the sum of their 17 opponents' Vegas win totals. League average is 144.5 total opponent wins. Higher = tougher slate. Red bars = above league avg, blue = below.
SoS · No. 02

Front- vs back-loaded.

H1 (W1–9) vs H2 (W10–18) opponent-quality swing.

  • Gets harder in the second half: PIT (+1.75), SF (+1.68), DET (+1.12)
  • Gets easier in the second half: DEN (-1.79), BUF (-1.78), WAS (-1.68)
  • 9 back-loaded · 8 front-loaded · 15 neutral schedules (±0.5 win-total threshold)
  • Useful for early-season power rankings and futures markets — front-loaded teams will look better than they are by mid-season
How to read X-axis = team's average opponent win total in weeks 1–9 (H1). Y-axis = same for weeks 10–18 (H2). The diagonal = equal difficulty in both halves. Above the line: harder H2 (records will fade). Below: easier H2 (records will improve).
SoS · No. 03

Schedule luck — Monte Carlo.

Is the "tough slate" real? 10 000 random-schedule sims per team.

  • Genuinely unlucky: BUF (97.7%ile) · CAR (94.6%) · TB (92.1%)
  • Genuinely lucky: DET (2.3%) · CLE (4.0%) · CIN (12.1%)
  • 8 teams unlucky, 4 lucky, 20 statistical noise
  • ~63% of "tough schedule" complaints don't survive a random-baseline test
How to read The 6 division games are locked by NFL rules. For each team I redraw their 11 non-division opponents 10 000 times from the 24-team non-division pool, then compute SoS. The percentile = where their actual SoS lands. 97% = harder than 97% of random draws (unlucky). 3% = lucky. 15–85% = noise.

Gauntlet detector.

3+ consecutive top-10 opponents (and the soft stretches).

  • Worst gauntlet: MIN W8–11 (DET, BUF, GB, SF) — avg opp WT 10.5
  • Easiest stretch: DEN W13–15 (MIA, NYJ, LV) — avg WT 5.17
  • Most fortunate: KC (6 easy weeks, 0 gauntlet)
  • Only 7 league-wide gauntlets total — schedule mostly avoids pile-ups
  • Others: ARI W9–11, IND W1–3, PHI W15–17, LAC W8–10, LA W12–14, CHI W15–17
How to read Each row = one team's 18-week schedule. Cell color = opponent tier by Vegas win total — red = top 10, blue = bottom 10, gray = middle, black = bye. A "gauntlet" = 3+ consecutive red cells. Teams sorted by longest gauntlet desc.
SoS · meta — but does it matter?

Does Schedule Strength actually matter?

Historical retrospective 2017–2024 (n = 256 team-seasons).

How to read Left: raw correlation of in-season SoS percentile vs actual wins is slightly positive (r ≈ +0.22) — counterintuitive, but explained by team-strength endogeneity (good teams cluster in tough divisions, so their SoS looks high). Center: regress wins on own preseason strength, then look at the SoS effect on the residual — the line is nearly flat. The partial SoS coefficient is +0.12 wins per 1-point SoS swing, statistically indistinguishable from zero. Right: by decile, even the hardest-vs-easiest tail difference is only ~0.4 residual wins. Bottom line: after you control for your own talent, schedule strength barely moves the needle — most of what looks like "schedule effects" is actually team-strength effects.

IISchedule Shape

Same weight,
different distribution.

Two teams can share an identical SoS and still have wildly different season arcs. Variance and rest — the texture of a slate — change how the same numbers cash out over eighteen weeks.

05 Schedule volatility   ·   06 Bye leverage & rest differential   ·   07 Schedule Leverage Index
Shape · No. 05

Schedule volatility.

Std of opponent quality — who has a boom/bust slate?

  • Most boom/bust: LAC (σ=2.48) · BUF (2.41) · SF / NYJ (2.36) · DEN (2.32)
  • Most steady: BAL (σ=1.38) · ATL (1.46) · HOU (1.58) · CAR / TB (1.59)
  • Volatile schedules → wider distribution of possible records (10-7 or 6-11 from the same projection)
  • Steady schedules → records cluster near projection (better win-total bets)
  • Wider opponent spread (max-min in win totals): 7.0 for NO, IND, CIN, DAL, BUF, LAC vs 4.0 for BAL
How to read For each team I compute the standard deviation of their 17 opponents' Vegas win totals. High σ = lots of variance — mixing cupcakes with contenders. Low σ = every opponent clusters near league average. Unlike Jaccard-on-shared-opponents (which mostly reproduces NFL scheduling rules), volatility varies meaningfully across teams.
Shape · No. 06

Bye leverage & rest differential.

When the bye lands · who plays the most opps coming off a bye.

  • Best net rest: CHI (+15), BUF (+14)
  • Worst: LAC (-24) · PHI (-15)
  • Opponents-off-bye: LAC faces 4 (worst); ARI faces zero
  • Early byes (W5–6): CAR, CIN, DET, KC, MIA, MIN
  • Late byes (W14): ARI, DAL
How to read Rest differential = Σ(own_rest_days − opp_rest_days) across 17 games, using nflverse days-since-last-game. Positive = team usually has more rest. Color = bye-week timing tier. "Opps off bye" = opponents arriving with 14 days rest.
Shape · No. 07 — composite

Schedule Leverage Index.

All-in-one environmental burden: rest · off-bye opps · body clock · travel · wind.

  • Toughest environment: LAC (80) · PHI (80) · JAX (75) · LV (73) · LA (73) · SF (71)
  • Easiest: CAR (26) · TEN (28) · DAL (31) · CHI (31) · MIN (31) · ATL (33)
  • Each team's top burden: LAC = rest deficit; SF = travel; LV = body clock; CLE = wind
  • Composite is independent of opponent quality — it isolates non-SoS schedule pain
  • Useful as a tiebreaker when two teams have similar implied-wins SoS but different schedule environments
How to read Five non-opponent schedule burdens — rest differential, opponents off bye, body-clock disadvantage games, travel miles, wind exposure — each percentile-ranked within the league so 100 = worst burden in that dimension. The leverage index is the equal-weighted average. "Driver" = the dimension contributing most to that team's score.

IIIEnvironment

Air miles, body clocks,
and weather.

Strength-of-schedule says nothing about the things a roster carries onto the field — the late-night flight back from London, the 10 a.m. body-clock kickoff in Philly, the November gusts off Lake Erie. Three angles.

07 Travel miles & time zones   ·   08 Body-clock map   ·   09 Wind exposure
Env · No. 07

Travel miles & time zones.

Great-circle from home stadium · neutral counts as travel for both teams.

  • Most-traveled: SF (18 946 mi) · LA (17 486) · JAX (15 947)
  • All three play overseas (Melbourne / London)
  • Least: CAR (4 317) · CLE (4 537) · CHI (5 314)
  • 16 of 32 teams play an international game
  • SF leads TZ burden at 28.0 absolute shift hours; BUF/JAX/MIA each have a back-to-back 1 500+ mile week
How to read Home games = 0 miles. Away/neutral games = great-circle (haversine) distance from home stadium to the actual venue. International games stack on top in a different shade. Time-zone shifts use UTC offsets at 2026-09-15 (avoids DST edge cases).
Env · No. 08

Body-clock map.

Kickoff converted to each team's home tz (jet-lag proxy).

  • Worst body-clock burden: LA & LV (4 disadvantage games each)
  • LAC, SF, DEN: 3 each — all 1pm ET = 10am PT body clock
  • International games at 9:30am local = ~3:30–4:30am body clock for US teams
  • West Coast teams structurally disadvantaged by early ET windows
  • "Advantage" column inflated by routine 4pm slots — see caveats
How to read For each game I take the local stadium kickoff time and convert it into the team's home time zone — that's their "body clock" kickoff. A 1pm-ET game in Philly = 10am-PT body clock for the visiting Rams. Any game ≥2h earlier than 1pm body-clock = disadvantage. International games hit ~3:30–4:30am body clock — the worst entries.
Env · No. 09

Wind exposure.

Historical climate per outdoor stadium via meteostat (2015–2024 daily).

  • Most-exposed: CLE (156.5) · CHI (128.4) · PIT · BUF · MIA
  • Huntington Bank Field is the wind capital — 5 of 6 windiest games leaguewide
  • CHI has 6 high-wind games in late-season months
  • Sheltered: ARI (25.5, 13 indoor games) · DAL · MIN · ATL · LV
  • 178 outdoor games leaguewide, 15 flagged as high-wind
How to read For every outdoor NFL stadium I pulled 10 years of daily wind data from meteostat, then averaged by calendar month. Each 2026 game gets the historical avg wind for its stadium in its game month. A team's "exposure" = sum across their 17 games. Domes and retractables count as 0.

Narrative · No. 11

International return-week dip.

Historical retrospective 2007–2025 — does it exist?

  • Return-week ATS cover: 51.4% vs 50.0% baseline (n=107)
  • Margin: +0.23 vs +0.00 baseline
  • Within 1 SE — the "international hangover" is essentially noise
  • Null result: don't fade return-week games on this basis
  • 2026 has 9 international games (record) → biggest future sample to revisit
How to read For every team that played in an international game from 2007–2025, I checked their performance in the very next regular-season game — specifically did they cover the Vegas spread, and what was the scoring margin. The "baseline" is the league-wide 50% cover rate / 0-point margin across all games. If the international game tires teams out, return-week numbers should sit clearly below baseline. They don't.

Fantasy football angles.

Appendix · A1 — defensive quality composite

Defensive Quality Composite.

A multi-source defensive rating — better than "last year's FPA" alone.

How to read Four defense signals blended: 3-yr weighted FPA (35%) — historical performance · cap spend on defense (25%) — talent investment, via OverTheCap · ESPN FPI defensive efficiency (25%) — model-projected current strength · Vegas season win total (15%) — overall team strength proxy. Each signal percentile-ranked within the league; weighted sum produces a 0–100 composite. The downstream position SoS slides use this composite as the defensive-quality input instead of FPA alone.
Appendix · A1b — by position (composite)

Position SoS — composite-adjusted.

Position-specific FPA blended with forward-looking signals: cap spend, FPI, Vegas WT.

  • Toughest RB schedules: CAR · BUF · LV · LAC · CHI
  • Easiest RB schedules: PHI · DET · SEA · CLE · MIN
  • Composite includes 2026 cap investment in defense + ESPN FPI + Vegas win total — not purely backward-looking on 2023-25 FPA
  • Position-specific FPA still drives the bulk (50% weight) — defensive scheme fit by position
  • Compare to the team-level Defensive Quality Composite (A1) for a single-number lens
How to read For each defense per position: blend 50% position-FPA (recency-weighted 50/30/20) + 20% cap spend on defense + 20% ESPN FPI + 10% Vegas win total — all percentile-ranked within the league. For each offense team, average their opponents' composite-adjusted defensive quality, then percentile-rank. Higher = tougher matchups for that position group.

Each team's top dynasty RB vs the run defenses they'll face across 17 games.

Each team's top dynasty TE vs the TE-defenses they'll face across 17 games.

Appendix · A2c — QB

QB schedule strength.

Each team's top dynasty QB vs the pass defenses they'll face across 17 games.

How to read For each team, average their opponents' QB-FPA-allowed (standard scoring: pass_yds×0.04 + pass_TD×4 − INT×2 + rush_yds×0.1 + rush_TD×6 − fumble×2), recency-weighted 50/30/20 across 2023–25, then percentile-rank within the league. Higher = tougher pass defenses on the slate. Headshot / label = team's top dynasty-value QB.
Appendix · A3

Game-script RB SoS.

Implied spreads → leading-share → RB volume ceiling.

  • Best RB game-script: BAL (9.24) — 8 games as 3+ pt favorite
  • SF, DET, PHI, SEA round out top 5
  • Worst: MIA (7.37), ARI, LV — 6–8 games as 3+ pt underdog
How to read Win-total differential between two teams → implied per-game point spread (0.4×Δwins + 2.5 HFA). Spread → win probability via logistic (P = 1/(1+e^(−spread/7))). Sum the win probabilities across 17 games = expected leading-share — roughly how many games the team plays with a lead. RBs benefit from leads.
Appendix · A4

Pace × pace projected play volume.

3-year baseline pace × opp def pace, with a Vegas-tilt for game-control effects.

  • Most volume: DAL (1 092) · HOU (1 082) · CHI · LA · DET
  • Least: LV (971) · MIA · NYJ · TEN · ARI
  • League avg 1 035; top-to-bottom spread now ~121 plays (~7 plays/game) — wider than before because the Vegas tilt amplifies the gap
  • Pass-catcher floor lift in high-pace high-game-total matchups (DAL, HOU lead)
  • Streamable defenses against the bottom-5 (limit volume = limit garbage points)
How to read Three-year recency-weighted (50/30/20) pace baseline: each team's offensive plays/game and defensive plays-allowed/game from 2023–25 PBP. For every 2026 game, blend (own offensive pace, opp's def pace allowed) and apply a Vegas tilt: combined win totals above league-average get a small upward play-volume nudge (game-control effect — better teams trade snaps faster). Sum across 17 = projected season volume.

In Closing

Headline takeaways.

If you only remember a dozen things from this brief.

Strength of schedule

  • Hardest: ARI, MIA, DAL, ATL, LA, CAR
  • Easiest: DET, NO, CIN, KC
  • 21-win spread between extremes
  • Most "tough schedule" narratives are noise (only 8 of 32 unlucky vs random)

Schedule shape

  • Back-loaded: PIT, SF, DET (sell-high on early records)
  • Front-loaded: DEN, BUF, WAS (slow start, real H2)
  • Only 7 league-wide 3+ game gauntlets
  • MIN W8–11 is the brutal one (DET/BUF/GB/SF)

Environment

  • SF/LA/JAX top travel burden (overseas games)
  • CLE/CHI/PIT/BUF most wind-exposed
  • West Coast teams structurally disadvantaged by body clock

Null results worth knowing

  • International return-week dip is statistical noise
  • ~63% of "tough schedule" complaints don't survive randomization
  • 25 of 32 teams have zero gauntlet stretches

Fine Print

Caveats & known limitations.

Where the data is thinner than the chart suggests.

  1. Win totals snapshot — Rotowire/DraftKings as of 2026-05-15, one day after schedule release. Lines will move materially before week 1. Re-run implied_wins_sos.py with refreshed totals to update every SoS-derived analysis.
  2. CB/WR matchup — PFR returned 403; agent fell back to PBP counting stats (deflections + INTs + tackles). Noisier than passer-rating-allowed. Ourlads scrape missed ARI. Treat as a coarse tier, not a precise ranking.
  3. Body-clock map — "Advantage" threshold catches routine 4pm and primetime slots, inflating the count. Disadvantage column is the trustworthy one.
  4. Revenge games — Uses 2025 rosters as a 2026 proxy. Late summer signings/cuts will shift the list.
  5. International return-week dip — Documented as a null result; effect within 1 SE of baseline (n=107).
  6. Schedule luck Monte Carlo — Draws non-division opponents with replacement. A without-replacement constraint would tighten the distribution slightly.

# Body-Clock Map — 2026 Schedule

- **LA carries the heaviest body-clock burden** with 4 disadvantage games (net -8). Examples: SF at LA week 1, 8:35pm Melbourne = 3:35am PT body clock; LA at PHI week 4, 1:00pm ET = 10:00am PT body clock; LA at WAS week 9, 1:00pm ET = 10:00am PT body clock.
- **Other West-Coast sufferers:** LV (4), LAC (3), and SF (3) round out the top tier. E.g., LV at NE week 5, 1:00pm ET = 10:00am PT body clock; LV at NYJ week 8, 1:00pm ET = 10:00am PT body clock; LAC at BUF week 3, 1:00pm ET = 10:00am PT body clock; SF at LA week 1, 8:35pm Melbourne = 3:35am PT body clock.
- **International games are the single worst body-clock hits.** Examples: HOU at JAX week 6, 9:30am London = 3:30am CT body clock; PIT at NO week 7, 9:30am Paris = 3:30am CT body clock. A 9:30am-local kick in London/Madrid/Berlin puts US teams at a 3–4am body-clock kickoff.
- **Worst purely-domestic kick:** LA at PHI week 4, 1:00pm ET = 10:00am PT body clock — the canonical 1pm-ET-vs-West-Coast disadvantage.
- **Net counts (32 disadvantage, 226 advantage league-wide):** Mountain/Central/Eastern teams almost never wake up early for kickoff, while West-Coast teams hit the 10am body-clock window 3–4 times each. The structural penalty is almost entirely a West-Coast problem driven by 1pm-ET windows.
